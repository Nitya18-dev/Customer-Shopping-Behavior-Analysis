# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Overview
This project delivers an end-to-end data analytics pipeline to uncover actionable insights into customer purchasing patterns. Using a transactional dataset of 3,900 purchases, the project tracks spending behavior, demographic segments, and product preferences to drive strategic business decisions.

The workflow covers **Data Cleaning & EDA** in Python, **Relational Database Storage & Querying** in PostgreSQL, and **Interactive Visualization** through a Power BI dashboard.

---

## Dataset Summary
The project analyzes a detailed dataset capturing customer demographics and purchasing habits:
* **Size:** 3,900 rows and 18 columns.
* **Demographics:** Age, Gender, Location, and Subscription Status.
* **Transaction Details:** Item Purchased, Category, Purchase Amount (USD), Size, Color, and Season.
* **Behavioral Metrics:** Review Rating, Shipping Type, Discount Applied, Promo Code Used, and Purchase Frequency.

---

## Technical Toolkit
* **Data Processing & EDA:** Python (`pandas`, `numpy`)
* **Database Management:** PostgreSQL
* **Business Intelligence:** Power BI Desktop

---

## Execution Steps

### 1. Python: Cleaning & Exploratory Data Analysis
* **Initial Exploration:** Evaluated dataset schema using `df.info()` and statistical summaries via `df.describe()`.
* **Missing Data Handling:** Imputed 37 missing values in the `Review Rating` column using the median rating of each specific product category.
* **Column Standardization:** Renamed all headers to clean `snake_case` for consistency.
* **Feature Engineering:** * Created an `age_group` column by binning customer ages.
  * Formatted a `purchase_frequency_days` column to standardize purchase cadence.
* **Data Ingestion:** Connected the Python script to PostgreSQL using SQLAlchemy and transferred the cleaned DataFrame directly into the database.

### 2. SQL: Structured Business Queries
Wrote optimized PostgreSQL queries to answer key business questions:
1. **Revenue by Gender:** Compared total revenue generated by male vs. female customers.
2. **High-Spending Discount Users:** Identified customers who used discounts but still spent above the average purchase amount.
3. **Top 5 Products by Rating:** Found products with the highest average review ratings.
4. **Shipping Type Comparison:** Compared average purchase amounts across different shipping methods.
5. **Subscribers vs. Non-Subscribers:** Analyzed average spend and total revenue across subscription tiers.
6. **Discount Dependencies:** Identified the top 5 products with the highest percentage of discounted purchases.
7. **Customer Segmentation:** Classified shoppers into New, Returning, and Loyal segments based on purchase frequency.
8. **Top Products per Category:** Listed the top 3 most purchased products within each category.
9. **Repeat Buyers Propensity:** Checked if repeat buyers (>5 purchases) are more likely to subscribe.
10. **Revenue by Age Group:** Calculated total revenue contribution for each demographic age group.

### 3. Power BI: Interactive Dashboard
Built a dynamic dashboard to visually present the extracted insights:
* **Core KPIs:** Displays total customers (3.9K), Average Purchase Amount (\$59.76), and Average Review Rating (3.75).
* **Subscription Breakdown:** A donut chart showing the split between subscriber types (27% Yes / 73% No).
* **Sales & Revenue Splits:** Bar charts comparing total sales volume and revenue across product categories and age groups.
* **Interactive Slicers:** Included dynamic filters for Gender, Category, Subscription Status, and Shipping Type for on-the-fly analysis.

---

## Key Insights & Recommendations
* **Focus on High-Value Segments:** Male consumers and the `Young Adult` age group contribute the highest total revenue. Focus marketing budgets on these segments.
* **Nurture Customer Retention:** Over 3,100 customers fall into the `Loyal` tier. Implement a loyalty reward system to transition the 701 `Returning` customers into this loyal bracket.
* **Review Discount Strategies:** Products like Hats and Sneakers have high discount dependencies (around 50%). Review promotional policies to protect profit margins.
